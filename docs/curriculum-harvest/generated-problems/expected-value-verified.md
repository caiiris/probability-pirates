## expected-value-0001 - Spinner payoff table average

- **Candidate:** EV-CAND-0001
- **Category:** simple payoff table expected value
- **Difficulty tag:** medium
- **Prompt:** A carnival spinner lands on red with probability 1/2, blue with probability 1/3, and gray with probability 1/6. Red pays $2.00, blue pays $0.50, and gray loses $1.00. What is the long-run average payoff per spin?
- **Answer:** $1.00 (100/1 cents)
- **Solver:** E = 200(1/2) + 50(1/3) - 100(1/6) cents
- **Skills:** expected-value, weighted-average, long-run-vs-single-trial, probability-distribution
- **Recognized taxonomy IDs:** long-run-vs-single-trial
- **Missing taxonomy IDs:** expected-value, weighted-average, probability-distribution
- **Misconception traps:** chooses-most-likely-outcome-as-average, ignores-negative-payoff, adds-payoffs-without-probability-weights
- **Passed:** yes

### Outcome Payoffs

- Red sector: probability 1/2, payoff 200/1 cents
- Blue sector: probability 1/3, payoff 50/1 cents
- Gray sector: probability 1/6, payoff -100/1 cents

### Verification

- Probability mass sums to 1/1.
- Exact expected payoff is $1.00 (100/1 cents).
- Negative gray spins are included as negative cents, not ignored.

- Risky Booth gross EV is 500/3 cents (5/3 dollars); net EV is -400/3 cents (-4/3 dollars).
- Safe Booth edge is 2525/9 cents (101/36 dollars).
